# VTPEH6270: Data Project
# From Kampala to Campus: A Comparative Analysis of Tech Engagement and Social-Emotional Health Outcomes

## Description
This project investigates the intersection of mobile health (mHealth) technology, AI engagement, and mental well-being across two distinct global populations. By comparing a localized clinical pilot in Uganda with a broad national survey in the US, this research explores how digital tools influence stress management, social interaction, and emotional health.

While initial exploratory work was conducted using the **mWel** dataset, the current and primary focus of this analysis utilizes the **Healthy Minds Study (HMS)** dataset to explore these dynamics across a broader, national student population.

## Research Question
How do patterns of technology and AI engagement correlate with social-emotional health outcomes among Ugandan educators (via mWEL) compared to US college students (via HMS)?

## Data Sources
1. **mWEL Pilot Study (NCT06087159 / s23-00600):** A clinical trial evaluating a personalized mHealth toolkit for stress management among 160 teachers and caregivers in Uganda.
2. **Healthy Minds Study (HMS):** A national, open-source dataset focused on mental health and technology utilization among adolescent and young adult students in the United States.

## Project Structure
- `data/raw/` → Original datasets (Note: mWEL data is de-identified).
- `data/processed/` → Cleaned data files and merged subsets for comparative analysis.
- `scripts/` → R and RMarkdown scripts for data wrangling, data analysis and simulations.
- `outputs/figures/` → Comparative visualizations (e.g., Tech use vs. Anxiety scores).
- `outputs/reports/` → Final analysis summaries and Checkpoint submissions.

## Methods
* **Statistical Analysis**: Utilizing R to identify correlations between tech engagement and mental health indicators.
* **Simulation**: Modeling potential social-emotional shifts based on increased AI tool use.
